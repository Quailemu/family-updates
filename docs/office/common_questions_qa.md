# Common questions (Q&A)

Q: Is this for urgent messages?  
A: No. It is for non-urgent, social messages only. For anything urgent, contact the care home directly.

Q: Will I see a history of messages?  
A: No. Only the current message is kept. Each new message replaces the previous one.

Q: Are messages private?  
A: Treat messages as shared within the care home. Staff may play messages in shared spaces.

Q: Can staff respond right away?  
A: Playback is arranged by the care home, so it may not be immediate.

Q: What if a family member or friend asks a care or safeguarding question?  
A: Ask them to contact the care home directly through normal care channels.

Q: Why can a family message disappear?  
A: A new message from that same contact replaces their previous one by design.

Q: Can one contact see another contact's messages?  
A: No. Other Family Members with Resident Access cannot access another Family Member's Family/Friend -> Resident message channel.

Q: Can we keep old messages for memory work?  
A: Not in this service. It is intentionally current-message only with no archive.

Q: Is this a live chat service?  
A: No. It is not real time and there are no read receipts or typing indicators.

Q: Who decides who gets Resident Access?  
A: The care home decides and manages Family Members with Resident Access.

Q: Can office users do mobile tasks?  
A: No. Care Hub - Office and Care Hub - Mobile are separate role-based app variants.

Q: Can mobile users access office documents?  
A: No. Care Hub - Mobile is restricted and does not include office/admin documents.

Q: What should staff do before sending a resident recording?  
A: Follow local process and confirm the message has been listened to before sending.

Q: What if a message sounds distressing or unsuitable?  
A: Follow the care home's safeguarding and escalation procedures immediately.

Q: Who handles complaints about care quality?  
A: The care home handles care complaints through its own complaints process.

Q: Who handles platform faults or login issues?  
A: The care home can raise technical support issues with voice-message.com.

Q: Does the platform moderate message content?  
A: No. The care home remains responsible for supervision and content management.

Q: Can this service be used for medical updates?  
A: No. Use normal care home channels for care or health information.

Q: Is consent handled by the platform?  
A: No. Consent, capacity, and authority checks are care-home responsibilities.

Q: Can a removed contact still access messages?  
A: Access should stop once removed, subject to current session and device controls.

Q: Should staff share office login details outside the care home?  
A: No. Shared operator credentials are internal and must be handled securely.

Q: Is two-factor authentication required?  
A: It may be optional or required by care-home policy; it is recommended for Office access.

Q: What if a resident is asleep or unavailable?  
A: Playback can wait. The service is designed for calm, non-urgent communication.

Q: Can staff edit a received family recording?  
A: No. Staff can play it or replace with a new recording only through normal flow.

Q: Where do contracts and templates live?  
A: In the Office "Contracts & templates" section.

Q: What is the safest short explanation to give families?  
A: "This is a non-urgent social voice-message service. It is not live and only the latest message is kept."
