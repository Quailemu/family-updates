## Purpose

This document sets out the family-side responsibilities when familyupdates.care is used at home.

familyupdates.care is for current, non-urgent family coordination. It helps make the Family Organiser role more workable by keeping present care, support, and communication bounded.

## Family Organiser role

The Family Organiser is usually in a hands-on role, as well as handling family communication and the admin of another person's life.

The Family Organiser may use the app to:

- share one current family update
- send specific organiser messages
- create simple practical requests
- review structured replies
- keep noticeboard-style information current
- invite Family Members and, where needed, Mobile Support

The Family Organiser is not agreeing to be available all the time, solve everything, or act as everyone's private messenger.

## Emergency protocol and essential information

The Family Organiser does not hold essential data or the emergency protocol inside familyupdates.care.

Those arrangements are managed separately by the family so the organiser can focus on present care, support, and communication.

The family should agree where emergency contact details, essential information, legal documents, medical information, financial information, and other private records are kept outside the app. The exact emergency protocol and how contact details are shared are decided by the family outside familyupdates.care.

## Mobile Support

Mobile Support is optional.

It is used where another person is also providing practical support through the app, paid or unpaid. That person may be a paid carer, professional support worker, spouse, family member, neighbour, or trusted helper.

Mobile Support is separate from ordinary Family Member access.

## Boundaries

familyupdates.care is non-urgent and not live.

The app does not:

- store emergency protocols or emergency contact lists
- store legal documents, medical records, financial records, or private family files
- replace urgent phone contact
- provide safeguarding, medical, legal, or financial advice
- manage complaints, disputes, or family decision-making
- create a family archive or live chat

Urgent, medical, safeguarding, legal, financial, private, or time-critical matters must use normal direct contact routes outside the app.
