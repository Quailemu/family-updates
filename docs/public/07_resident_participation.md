# Resident participation

## Purpose

This document explains how residents may take part in voice messaging, and how participation is supported by care staff within the care home setting.

## How residents take part

Residents can take part in sending and receiving social, non-urgent voice messages with support from care staff. The care home decides when and how residents participate, and staff assist with recording and playback.

If a resident chooses to send a message to family, this is managed by a carer using Care Hub – Mobile. The resident decides whether to send a message; carers operate the app on their behalf.

Participation is supervised and managed by the care home. This helps ensure the service is used in line with the home’s usual routines and responsibilities.

## Message content and expectations

Messages are social and non-urgent. Resident messages may include conversational context, but they should not be relied upon for care or health information.

Only the most recent voice message is kept and played. Older messages are automatically replaced. There is no message history.

## Privacy and setting

Messages are recorded and played within the care home environment. They are intended for the resident and their family or friends, but they are not guaranteed to be private in the care-home setting.
